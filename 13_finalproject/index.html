<div class="textcontainer"> <p class="margin"></p> <h3>Final Project: Automated Origami Folding Machine</h3> <p class="margin"></p> <h4>Purpose of My Project</h4> My final project aims to automate the process of folding origami. The purpose is to explore how a machine can create a clean, consistent crease using mechanics and electronics, which turns out to be much more complex than it looks. <p class="margin"></p> <h4>From MVP Week to Final Project</h4> From MVP week, I realized I needed a stronger hinge, better torque, and a structure that wouldn’t shift. For the final project, I redesigned the hinge, used a stronger servo, added a holding mechanism, and rebuilt the system using plywood boxes instead of cardboard. <p class="margin"></p> <h4>Better Hinge Design</h4> I redesigned the hinge to increase wall thickness, improve tolerances, and prevent cracking. <p class="margin"></p> <img src="./final_hinge.jpg" alt="Final hinge design" width="400" height="400"> <p class="margin"></p> <p class="margin"></p> <h4>Stronger Servo and Screw Attachment</h4> I upgraded to a stronger servo and switched to a screw-mounted arm for a more secure connection. <p class="margin"></p> <img src="./comparison.jpg" alt="Holding mechanism prototypes" width="400" height="400"> <p class="margin"></p> <p class="margin"></p> <video width="640" height="360" autoplay muted> <source src="paper_fold_basic.mp4" type="video/mp4"> Your browser does not support the video tag. </video> <p class="margin"></p> <h4>Thread Linkage and Holding Mechanism</h4> One issue in the MVP was that I needed to tape the paper onto the hinge for every fold. This prevented the system from being semi-autonomous. So I designed a holding arm mechanism for a second servo, and shifted to a hinge with a larger gap so the mechansim could sit in between. <p class="margin"></p> <img src="./holding_mechanism_screw.jpg" alt="Holding mechanism prototypes" width="400" height="400"> <p class="margin"></p> <p class="margin"></p> <img src="./between_hinge.jpg" alt="Holding mechanism prototypes" width="400" height="400"> <p class="margin"></p> I also attached a thread between the folding servo arm and hinge that could bring the hinge down after every fold. <h4>Paper Choice and Support Box</h4> Thick paper did not fold well because the folding arm was open on one end. <p class="margin"></p> <video width="640" height="360" autoplay muted> <source src="thick_paper_fail.mp4" type="video/mp4"> Your browser does not support the video tag. </video> <p class="margin"></p> Normal printer paper performed much better. <p class="margin"></p> <video width="640" height="360" autoplay muted> <source src="printer_paper_pass.mp4" type="video/mp4"> Your browser does not support the video tag. </video> <p class="margin"></p> However, I had to hold the motors down. I later upgraded to a wider holding mechanism. <p class="margin"></p> <img src="./wider_holding.jpg" alt="Holding mechanism prototypes" width="400" height="400"> <p class="margin"></p> <h4>Electronics and Code</h4> I switched from an ESP32 to a Xiao board, since my ESP32 somehow broke in between. This gave me good soldering practice. I controlled two servos: one for holding and one for folding. This is the code that I used: <p class="margin"></p> <pre><code> #include &lt;ESP32Servo.h&gt; Servo myservofold; // create servo object to control a servo Servo myservohold; int Fpos = 0; int Hpos = 120; // variable to store the servo position. int ButtonPinFold = D7; int ServoPinFold = D8; int ServoPinHold = D9; void setup() { myservofold.attach(ServoPinFold); myservofold.write(Fpos); myservohold.attach(ServoPinHold); myservohold.write(Hpos); pinMode(ButtonPinFold, INPUT_PULLUP); Serial.begin(9600); } void loop() { int ButtonStateFold = digitalRead(ButtonPinFold); Serial.println(ButtonStateFold); if (ButtonStateFold == LOW) { myservohold.write(175); delay(1000); myservofold.write(118); delay(1000); myservohold.write(175); delay(1000); myservofold.write(118); delay(1000); myservofold.write(0); delay(1000); myservohold.write(120); delay(1000); } } </code></pre> <p class="margin"></p> <h4>Moving from Cardboard to Plywood</h4> Cardboard wasn’t strong enough, and since the motors were just secured with double sided tape they kepy moving a lot. I decided to shift to finger-joint plywood boxes. I prototyped measurements using cardboard and then laser-cut 6mm plywood using MakerCase. The 0.18mm kerf made the finger joints tight enough to hammer-fit without glue. <p class="margin"></p> <img src="./cardboard_prototype.jpg" alt="Cardboard prototype" width="400" height="400"> <p class="margin"></p> <p class="margin"></p> <img src="./mallet_box_fit.jpg" alt="Mallet press-fit plywood box" width="400" height="400"> <p class="margin"></p> <h4>Motor Mounts and L-Joints</h4> I took measurements of the motor and designed a L-joint to hold it down. I drilled on the plywood to fit screws through it to mount the motors securely. Experimenting with different measurements took some time. <p class="margin"></p> <img src="./L-joint.jpg" alt="Sanding plywood edges" width="400" height="400"> <p class="margin"></p> <p class="margin"></p> <img src="./Screws.jpg" alt="Sanding plywood edges" width="400" height="400"> <p class="margin"></p> <p class="margin"></p> <img src="./mountedL-joint.jpg" alt="Sanding plywood edges" width="400" height="400"> <p class="margin"></p> After sanding the plywood, I attached the boxes together and taped the hinge onto the surface. <p class="margin"></p> <img src="./assembled_boxes.jpg" alt="Assembled plywood boxes" width="400" height="400"> <p class="margin"></p> <h4>Switch Mount and Base</h4> I soldered a switch and 3D printed a small mount so it could be pressed from the front. Then I attached the whole mechanism onto a plywood base for stability. <p class="margin"></p> <h4>Final Project Demo</h4> <p class="margin"></p> <video width="640" height="360" autoplay muted> <source src="final_fold_demo.mp4" type="video/mp4"> Your browser does not support the video tag. </video> <p class="margin"></p> Here is my full presentation video: <br> <a href="https://youtu.be/vIY8vMq3GZU?si=BjYDIQr3U0yvIIMN">Link to watch it</a> <p class="margin"></p> <h4>Further Work</h4> <ul> <li>Reduce the hinge gap for sharper folds</li> <li>Thin the holding mechanism while keeping it stable</li> <li>Add a tiny motorized roller to advance the paper</li> <li>Explore multiple sequential folds</li> </ul> <p class="margin"></p> <h4>End Thoughts on the Term</h4> I am very proud of how this project turned out.
